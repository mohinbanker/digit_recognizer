This is an experiment studying behavior in a simple game. In this game you will play the role of either a “buyer” or a “seller”.

If you are a seller, you will earn tokens by producing objects and selling them to buyers.

If you are a buyer, you will earn tokens by buying an object from a seller and then giving it to the experimenter in exchange for tokens.

You will play this game many times. Each time you play the game, we call this a “round”.

Each round, you will be grouped with {{ subsession.buyers|add:subsession.sellers|add:"-1"}} other participant{{ subsession.buyers|add:subsession.sellers|add:"-1"|pluralize }}. There will be {{ subsession.sellers }} seller{{ subsession.sellers|pluralize }}. There will be {{ subsession.buyers}} buyer{{ subsession.buyers|pluralize }}.

Who is in your group and what role you play are determined randomly by the computer at the start of each round.

Each round works like this:

  1. The computer randomly groups participants into groups of {{ subsession.buyers|add:subsession.sellers }}.
  2. The computer randomly assigns {{ subsession.buyers }} buyer role{{ subsession.buyers|pluralize }} and {{ subsession.sellers }} seller role{{ subsession.sellers|pluralize }} in each group.
  3. Sellers set the prices of their objects.
  4. Buyers see the prices of the sellers in their group.
  5. Each buyer chooses a seller to buy from and pays the seller's price in tokens.
  6. Each seller produces the number of objects that they sold (between 0 and {{ subsession.buyers }}), and gives one to each of their buyers.
  7. Each buyer gives the object they bought to the experimenter in exchange for tokens.
